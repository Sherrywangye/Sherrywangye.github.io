Supervised learning

1.Linear Regression

As an initial choice: y as a linear function of x:

$h{\theta}(x{i})=\theta^T*x_{i}$

The cost function:

$J(\theta)=\frac{1}{2}(h{\theta}(x{i})-y_{i})^2$

But why we choose the least-square cost function $J(\theta)$?
here is the reason:
Let’s assume that the target variable and input variables are related via the below equation:

$y{i}=\theta^Tx{i}+\epsilon_{i}$

$\epsilon{i}$ refers to either unmodeled effects (such as if there are some features very pertinent to predicting housing price, but that we’d left out of the regression), or random noise.
**$\epsilon{i}$ is ǫ(i) are distributed IID（就是它了，传说中的独立同分布） (independently and identically distributed) accoding to Gaussian distributon**(注意用法，满足..分布并且独立同分布的说法：be distributed IDD according to Gaussian distributon),即

$\epsilon_{i}\sim N(0,\delta^2)$
$p(\epsilon{i})=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\delta}}exp(-\frac{\epsilon{i}^2}{2\delta^2})$

所以是在正态分布$\epsilon{i}$上加了一个$\theta^Tx$，这个时候整个$y{i}$服从：

$y_{i}\sim N(\theta^Tx,\delta^2)$)
$p(y{i} |x{i};\theta)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\delta}}exp(-\frac{(y-\theta^Tx)^2}{2\delta^2})$

现在想知道，出现整个已知标签$Y=(y{1}…y{m})$的概率，显然独立同分布用乘法

$L(\theta)=\prod{i=1}^{m}p(y{i} | x{i};\theta)=\prod{i=1}^{m}\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\delta}}exp(-\frac{(y{i}-\theta^Tx{i})^2}{2\delta^2})$
$LogL(\theta)=Log\prod{i=1}^{m}\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\delta}}exp(-\frac{(y{i}-\theta^Tx{i})^2}{2\delta^2})=mLog\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\delta}-\frac{1}{2\delta^2}*(y{i}-\theta^Tx)^2$

由于$mLog\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\delta}$为常数，$\theta$也是常数，则最大化$LogL(\theta)$只需最小化

$\frac{1}{2}(y{i}-\theta^Tx)^2=\frac{1}{2}(h{\theta}(x{i})-y{i})^2$

然而最小化的方法有许多种，必须掌握的是gradient descent 和Normal equation
